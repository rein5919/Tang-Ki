    Illuminated by the subdued hues of red, the almighty’s altar
    was greeted by a juvenile.

    With a dying cigarette clasped between his fingers, he enthroned himself on a large wooden chair, denoting the commencement of the celestial ritual.

    Settled in his royal throne, his eyelids grew heavy and there was no attempt to resist – acquiring a full state of meditation.



    The silence in the room was broken by rhythmic collisions.

    He rocked back and forth, ensuring the stiff timber frame
    met his shoulders each time.

    His trusty assistants stood close by, ensuring his safety.

    In a flash, the possession came to a halt.

    Standing upright, with his hands raised in a hypnotic manner, he was unrecognisable. The man had transcended mortality, possessed by his owner from an alternate realm. He had fulfilled his duty as a spirit-medium, fully embodying the Hell Deity, Da Er Ye Bo.

    Tangki is a common practice in Taoism. 26-year-old Ming is regarded as a Tangki master within the Taoist community. He earned his prestige after being in the practice for over a decade, and has even taken on students under his wing.

    As one of the 2,000 Sintuas [1] in Singapore, Ming's temple is a humble unit in a factory building which offers consultation services on Saturday nights.

    [1] Sintuas are temples located within public housing flats.

    Every week, Ming dons a striking traditional costume to fully express his Deity, Da Er Ye Bo, in human-form. When the Tangki wears the costume of the God and theatrically behaves like the God, he is an image of the God [2].

    Crowned in a stubby golden headdress with a long silken braid, he wields an enamelled, box-like plaque. Emboldened with a fanciful Chinese script that reads “Good and Evil Clearly Differentiated”, this serves as a warning to anyone who dares violate the law or commit crimes.

    With his fierce face and no-nonsense disposition, clients can be certain that their session with the Hell Deity will be the cure to their ailments. Besides ensuring law and order is maintained in the underworld, he fulfills his tasks on Earth by helping humans through Tangki.

    [2] Chan, 2015

    Image captions

      Titled as devotees in this practice, clients came with differing concerns in mind. They sought to resolve all kinds of enquiries, ranging from wealth, family, academics, health and even seemingly trivial day-to-day problems.

      In the face of the glitz and glamour, the Hell-sent Deity entered our world with a strong resolve.
      An old figure, he spoke with a grumpy mellow voice and began his meticulous routine,
      preparing himself for the upcoming client meeting.

      Settled into the medium as his earth-bound vessel, he inched towards his workstation, close enough for his legs to be tucked under the draping tablecloth. This dual-purpose fabric served as a decorative ornament and as a gateway of communication.

      The Deity’s foot soldiers went on daily patrols, collecting intelligence on humans everywhere. Upon hearing the call of a rustling abacus, the troopers rushed back with their findings, using this channel to keep their Deity informed during consultations.

      All consultations started with an “email”.
      Traditional Chinese talisman, Fulu [3], resembles our modern day messaging platform.

      With his steady paintbrush doused in black ink, he calligraphically crafted messages on each talisman, opening each client’s case.

      His assistants brought the stack of solid-coloured talismans and blessed them at the altar, sending off his careful messages to the Deity’s superior.

      [3] Fulu is a term for Taoist incantations and magic symbols, written or painted as talismans.

      This Deity-assistant dynamic flowed seamlessly as they were met with their first client.

      “Hello can help me please? My mother’s leg very
      pain and I don’t know what to do already..”

      As the client sat beside the Deity,
      unsure of where to begin, she
      was met with a calm, reassuring voice.
      A stark contrast from the
      fierce, dark face of Da Er Ye Bo.

      “Tell me more about your mother’s condition. Do not be afraid, I am here to help you.”



      Startled by the all-knowing Deity, the client took a few deep breaths and recentred herself before explaining her predicament.



      “My mother is very stubborn. Cannot walk already but still don’t want see doctor.”

      Image captions

        Listening attentively to the client's woes, he opened the case. Momentarily, the conversation came to a stand-still as he flicked the abacus beads in front of her, deep in thought as he deliberated solutions for his client.

        Da Er Ye Bo reached across the table for the Hulu [4], a gourd-shaped bottle. Holding two fingers over the Hulu, he closed his eyes and prayed under his breath, blessing its contents with healing capabilities.

        “Every night, warm the ointment in the bottle, and rub it over the area that causes the most pain. If you need more help, don’t hesitate to come back.”

        [4] Hulu is a gourd-shaped bottle capable of being blessed with healing abilities.

        With his blessings, this ointment would give her mother a long-awaited night free from pain. Holding the Hulu in her outstretched palms, she let out a sigh of relief only to be interrupted by the Hell Deity.

        “Your health isn’t doing too well either, you know? You haven’t been eating and sleeping properly.”

        The client paused in shock.

        How did he know?

        Taking on the role of an advisor and father figure, the Deity reminded her to prioritise her health and happiness. In a stern but comforting tone, Da Er Ye Bo imparted lifelong values onto the client. 

        With his final parting words of wisdom, 

        “Take care of yourself, first - before worrying about others.” 

        She nodded reassuringly and thanked the Deity, having finally been granted a resolve for the issues that had been weighing her down for so long.

        With a slam, a final talisman was sent off and the procession ended. Da Er Ye Bo took a final gulp of beer, bidding farewell to his assistant, before leaving his earth-bound host.









        Loud, shrieking laughter greeted Ming as his soul returned to his body.


        Chen, a 23-year-old medium in practice, sat at the other side of the room. Warehouse assistant by day, spirit-medium by night, he works tirelessly assisting Ming and channels a youthful Deity.

        Image captions

          “Am I not cute?” he exclaimed in his notably high-pitched voice, pointing at the statue of himself by the altar.

          The bellowing of an infant’s voice signalled the arrival of the beloved Child Deity. Nibbling away on one of his many pacifiers, Shan Cai Tong Zi graced the room with his buoyant presence.

          This particular Deity is truly one-of-a-kind. Regardless of the medium he possesses, he remains clearly discernible due to his peculiar mannerisms.

          As an infant, he indulges in sweet drinks and candies to his liking. Prancing around the room in a crimson bib, he charitably offers treats to his devotees. His cheeky nature and acts of mischief have never failed to light up a dull room.

          This jovial demeanour often overshadows his wisdom. Crowned as the Child Deity of Wealth, he has been empowered with the ability to make precise lottery number predictions.

          This innate skill allows him to administer a fortune to individuals, making financial issues look like a thing of the past.


          However, such liberties are not granted to any mere soul. A preliminary study of his devotee enables the lottery master to make an informed decision.

          If he concludes that you are worthy, he will present the accurate number digits to bless you. Conversely, the unworthy are rewarded with witty pranks from the jester himself, leaving the session with no monetary gains.










          The bustling atmosphere simmered down as the last devotee said his goodbyes. After a long night of consultations, the Child Deity still had a spring in his step. Despite his endearing enthusiasm, he snuggled into the crevices of his seat and gradually fell into a meditative state.

          Having fulfilled his responsibilities, he focused on bringing Chen’s wandering soul back. With a shake of Chen’s motionless body, the Child Deity ascended to his rightful throne, ending the possession.









          Accompanied by the gentle brushing of feathers on his arms and the chatter of his friends around him, Chen settled back into his body.


          Lighting a cigarette as he removed the large hat and loosened the collar of his shirt, he ruminated over the day’s events. Being a medium was tough, especially on his mind and body, but seeing the thankful smiles of his clients always spurred him on.

          With the sea of twinkling faces in mind, the medium reminisced on how his journey began.

          This radical passion for Tangki started at a young age. Intrigued by the unique tradition, he awaited his calling, yearning for the opportunity to serve as a medium.

          All that changed when a Deity visited him in his dreams.

          Bright, white light. A pair of well-worn shoes.
          A loud, booming voice asked for his devotion to serve as a medium.

          In his recurring dreams, he knelt on the ground as the question reverberated in his heart and mind. No matter how hard he tried, he could not look up to see the face of the figure, nor give a response. 

          Confused by his dreams, he approached his master for advice. Under Ming’s careful guidance, he embarked on his journey to becoming a medium - which began with seeking approval from his ancestors and parents.

          Given the toll of a medium’s work and the lack of a stable income, there was much to worry about. His parents incessantly questioned his plans for marriage and his career, ensuring he was certain of his decision. Even so, his growing confidence and devotion in his religion eventually led to his parents’ full support. 

          With his parents’ blessing, he proceeded to visit the shrine dedicated to his ancestors. Staring intently at the images of his predecessors, he thought long and hard about his question.

            I want to fulfill my life’s purpose as a Tangki medium.
            Will you support me in this decision?

            Clasping the Jiaobei nervously in his palms, he held them against his forehead, eyes clamped shut in anticipation.

            This was his lifelong goal.

            [5] Jiaobei are wooden divination tools, which are thrown in pairs to seek divine guidance in the form of a yes or no question.





            Apprehensive, he took a deep breath before hurling the two wooden blocks.

            He watched in slow motion as they fell to a resounding YES.

            Image captions

              Feeling the weight lifted from his shoulders, he thanked them profusely, reassuring them that he would make them proud.

              Everything was set in stone. Backed by the approvals,
              there was only one final step left.

              He visited Geok Hong Tian Temple, the first Taoist temple in Singapore, to seek approval from The Jade Emperor, the supreme ruler of Heaven. One had to gain his approval in order to practise Tangki, given that a medium’s role is crucial in bridging the spiritual and earthly realms.

              In a trance, the Emperor spoke to him.

              He remained silent for a long time and as his master placed a reassuring hand on his shoulder, he was reminded of his arduous journey. 

              The hardships of convincing his parents.

              The yearning to relieve his clients’ pains. 

              The tears of gratitude on their smiling faces. 

              He took a breath. There was no hesitation, he was ready. 

              That was two years ago, and he has had no regrets since.
